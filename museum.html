Illustrating Immigration

A prototype for a museum kiosk done for a class project. This kiosk was designed with the National Archives in mind, and uses images and posters from their collections to explore how immigration has been discussed in American history.

Project Overview

The goal of this project was to make an interactive prototype for a museum kiosk that worked in tandem with an exhibit. I chose to make a kiosk that fit with some of the themes in the Records of Rights exhibit at the National Archives. I used comics, posters, and illustrations to give an overview of American perceptions of immigration over time.

This project explored the concept of scale, and gave me an opportunity to think about interaction outside of the typical mobile or desktop context.

Project Details

Challenges

One of the main challenges I encountered had to do with the large scale of the kiosk. I wanted to ensure that I could fit all of the information I needed under each section, but the height of the screen meant that most of the navigation had to be in the middle so that people could reach it easily.

The pages for the individual images were the most challenging in this respect. I wanted the image to be the focal point of the page, but I also needed to put text underneath them. I settled on highlighting different portions of the image with buttons, and having each button correspond with a different fact.
